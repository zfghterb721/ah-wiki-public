# Pokemon League Guidelines

## What You Will Need
To run a Pokémon League, you need:
- A Professor who has passed their tournament organizer test.
- Access to the League page, which can be accessed by the organizer who owns the League.
- Correct promotional material from Pokémon.

### Reporting
#### Reporting Players on Pokemon.com:
- Access your Pokemon.com account and navigate to the "League" section.
- Add players who have attended your league sessions during the current month with their Pokemon IDs.
- Submit the player report by the 7th of the following month.

#### Printing Player ID Sheets:
- Go to Pokemon.com and log in to your account.
- Find the "Organizer" tabs or section on the website.
- Locate the option to download player ID sheets.
- Download and print player ID sheets for any new players who attend your league.

#### Managing Player Records:
- Keep a separate spreadsheet to record the details of all Pokemon players who attend your league sessions.
- Ensure that this spreadsheet is regularly updated with the names and Pokemon IDs of new players.

### Sanctioning
#### Sanctioning a League Challenge:
- Log in to the tournament sanctioning website: [Tournament Sanctioning Website](https://play-tools.pokemon.com/login).
- Choose "League Challenge" as the event type.
- Select a date for the League Challenge at least 14 days in advance from the current date.
- Fill out the necessary details for the event.
- Submit the sanctioning request for the League Challenge.

#### Sanctioning a League Cup:
- Follow steps 1 and 2 from the previous section.
- Choose "League Cup" as the event type.
- Select a date for the League Cup at least 14 days in advance from the current date.
- Fill out the necessary details for the event.
- Submit the sanctioning request for the League Cup.

#### Sanctioning GLC Events or Weekly Tournaments:
- Follow steps 1 and 2 from the previous sections.
- Choose the event type (GLC event or Standard tournament).
- Fill out the necessary details for the event.
- Submit the sanctioning request for the event.

#### Sanctioning Pre-Releases:
- Keep an eye on your email for notifications regarding pre-releases.
- Click on the provided link in the email to access the sanctioning form.
- Fill out the required information for the pre-release event.
- Submit the sanctioning request for the pre-release.

### TOM (Tournament Organizer Manager)
TOM is Pokémon's tournament software. Follow these steps to use it:
- Download TOM from your organizer tabs.
- Start a new tournament, select the tournament mode, and add the correct sanctioned ID.
- Follow the instructions provided by the tournament software to run the tournament.

### Reporting Tournaments
- After the tournament ends, upload results to the page where you sanctioned the event.
- Save the tournament results from TOM to your computer then click the upload TDF.

### Promotional Material
- Ensure you are reporting correctly and consistently to receive the most promotional material possible.
- Main source of promos: Pokémon Play Prize packs for most tournaments.
- League Cup Playmats for winners in each age division of a Pokémon Cup.
