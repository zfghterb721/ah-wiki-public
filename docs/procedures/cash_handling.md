# Cash Handling
Handling cash is an important part of working in retail, despite the popularity of credit cards and other modern payment methods, cash is still the preferred tender of choice for many customers. In this document we will look at how we handle cash, and the procedures put in place to protect yourself, fellow employees, and the business.

Cash Locations
==============

There are three locations where it is acceptable to store the business' cash:

Cash Drawer
-----------

During operating hours, cash will be stored in the **Cash Drawer** underneath the Point of Sale. It is not acceptable for cash to be stored here overnight (with the exception of loose coins) or when the retail floor is not occupied by a team member. Cash and change should be organized by denomination inside the drawer.

Deposit Drop Box
----------------

Deposit slips are placed inside the **Deposit Drop Box** each night after close. Only managers authorized to make bank deposits are allowed to open the deposit drop box. This contains all cash profits between deposit times and is therefore the most secure location for storing money.

Cash Lock Box
-------------

The **Cash Lock Box** is where on hand cash is stored during non operation hours. The key for this box is to be stored in the till, but the box itself should be place locked storage in the back of the business. During business hours this box can be kept at locked at the till in a drawer or an out of sight location. Do not leave the **Cash Lock Box** at the till when it is not occupied. When opening for the day, a **Cash Bag** inside this box will contain the opening cash. Outside of the **Cash Bag**, there is an area that contains no more than **$100** worth of coins, coin rolls and small bills. On hand cash can be exchanged for smaller denominations here, or small bills can be exchanged for any large bills before placing them in a nightly deposit slip.

Opening Shift
=============

-   For the opening shift count the paper money inside money bag that is in the Cash Lock Box. Do not worry about the small bills, coins, and coin rolls that are inside the box but not in the money bag. This should total $400.

-   Next, count the coins inside the Cash Drawer, this should be a small amount and is recorded under the C**hange** section of the OPEN/CLOSE sheet. This is then added to the total paper money counted above as the **Total.**

-   Enter the total amount into the POS under "Opening Float"

-   Place the Cash Lock (that now only includes change) underneath the POS counter.

Closing Shift
=============

-   For the closing shift, first count all of the cash and change inside the Cash Drawer. This amount will be what you enter into the POS as your End of Day count and also write down under total on the OPEN/CLOSE sheet. Count what you have tallied for coins and place that under the change column.

![image.png](/files/nhdummwnao.png)

![image.png](/files/znzlfqfuac.png)

-   After you have counted out the total money from the cash drawer, it's time to make a deposit slip. First, count out $400, and try to have it in the following denominations. If you need to buy change from the Cash Lock Box now is the time to do so. Make sure you only take out the same amount you put in.
  
  | Bills | Number of Bills     |
  | ------- | ---- |
  | $1      | 50   |
  | $5      | 30   |
  | $20     | 10   |
  | Total   | $400 |

-   Once you've counted out the $400, place that inside the **Money Bag** and place that inside the Cash Lock Box. Leave loose change inside the Cash Drawer. The remaining cash is your deposit. If you notice any large bills change area of the Cash Lock Box exchange them for $1s or $5s before making the deposit. Once money has been deposited it is no longer possible to buy change from it.

    Add up the deposit on the deposit slip. Sign your name on this slip, and write the amount under the deposit column of the OPEN/CLOSE sheet.

![image.png](/files/mxowtnhbgy.png)

-   You should now have a deposit, a Cash Lock Box, and a small amount of loose coinage in the Cash Drawer. Take the deposit and lock box to the staff room. Drop the deposit inside Deposit Drop Box and place the Cash Lock Box in your locations designated location.

Buying Change
=============

-   If you need to buy change during store hours you may do so by exchanging equal amounts from the Cash Drawer into the Cash Lock Box. For example if you are low on $1 bill you can place a $20 in the Cash Lock Box and pull out 20x $1 bills. If there isn't enough change in the Cash Lock Box you may call your manager for instructions on how to retrieve money from the Deposit Drop Box.

Notes
=====

Money counting isn't a difficult job, and as long as the processes are followed properly, there shouldn't be much issue. Miscounts happen, and when logged and documented are easily resolved without much effort. Failure to follow documentation procedures, however, can result in untracked discrepancies which cause wasted effort in reconciling, and potentially termination of employment depending on the severity of accounting errors. This is why it's very important to focus on the task and ensure accuracy . If you see something that looks off, report it to your manager immediately!

Deposits
--------

Deposits must be made on a weekly basis by someone approved by ownership to make deposits. The access code must be used to open the deposit box in order to bring money to the bank. When making a deposit a deposit slip must be filled out with the correct amount which should be the sum of all of the deposit envelopes from the deposit box. The store name MUST be written on the deposit slip. Place the cash and the deposit slip in the money bag and bring to the designated bank to make the deposit.
