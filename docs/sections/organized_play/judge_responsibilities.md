# Judge Responsibilities

## Purpose

This document defines the responsibilities and expectations of the event judge or event lead during organized play at A&H Games.

The judge is responsible for maintaining control, fairness, and consistency during the event.

---

## Role Definition

The judge (or event lead):

- Runs the event
- Makes rulings
- Enforces rules and policies
- Maintains the event environment

The judge is the primary authority during the event.

---

## Authority

- The judge has authority over all event-related decisions
- All player disputes must go through the judge
- Players may not bypass the judge to seek alternate rulings

Managers have final authority when escalation is required.

---

## Core Responsibilities

The judge must:

- Start and run the event on time
- Ensure the event is functioning correctly in the official system
- Make rulings when disputes arise
- Enforce all store policies during the event
- Maintain control of the play environment
- Ensure fair and consistent treatment of all players

---

## Rulings

### Making Rulings

- Listen to both players involved
- Gather necessary information
- Make a clear and immediate decision

Do not:
- Delay rulings unnecessarily
- Allow players to argue indefinitely
- Allow players to resolve disputes without oversight

---

### Consistency

- Apply rulings consistently across all players
- Do not change rulings mid-event without escalation
- Do not allow different standards for different players

---

### Finality

- Once a ruling is made, it stands unless escalated
- Do not debate rulings after a decision is made

---

## Escalation

Escalate to a manager when:

- A ruling cannot be confidently made
- A player refuses to accept a ruling
- A situation involves removal from the event
- A ban may be necessary
- The situation involves safety concerns
- There is uncertainty on how to proceed

Manager decisions are final.

---

## Policy Enforcement

All store policies apply during events.

See:
- ../policies/customer_conduct.md
- ../policies/family_friendly.md
- ../policies/store_safety.md

### Requirements

- Address violations immediately
- Follow enforcement steps defined in policy
- Do not ignore behavior because an event is running

---

## Player Behavior During Events

The judge must:

- Monitor player behavior throughout the event
- Address disruptive or inappropriate behavior immediately
- Maintain a safe and family-friendly environment

Players may be removed from the event if behavior does not improve.

---

## Player Removal

If a player must be removed:

- Stop the match immediately
- Inform the player they are removed from the event
- Do not argue or negotiate
- Escalate to a manager if necessary

Removed players:
- Are dropped from the event
- Do not receive a refund

---

## Event Integrity

The judge must ensure:

- Matches are played fairly
- Results are reported accurately
- Pairings are handled correctly

Do not:
- Allow players to manipulate results
- Allow intentional rule violations
- Modify results outside of proper procedures

---

## System Management

The judge must:

- Use the correct official system for the event
- Enter players correctly
- Record results accurately
- Ensure the event progresses properly

Errors must be corrected immediately.

---

## Time Management

- Start rounds promptly
- Keep the event moving
- Do not allow unnecessary delays

Late players:
- Are handled according to event standards
- Receive losses for missed rounds

---

## Employee Conduct

The judge must:

- Remain professional at all times
- Stay neutral in all situations
- Avoid favoritism

Do not:
- Participate in arguments
- Show bias toward any player
- Create exceptions to rules

---

## Playing in Events

- Judges may not play in events they are running unless explicitly approved

If approved:
- Responsibilities as judge take priority
- Event integrity must not be compromised

---

## Summary

- The judge runs the event and makes rulings
- All disputes go through the judge first
- Policies must be enforced during events
- Escalate when necessary
- Maintain fairness, consistency, and control

This role is enforceable. Failure to fulfill these responsibilities is a violation.
