# Organized Play Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the non-negotiable standards that apply to all organized play at A&H Games.

These standards ensure consistency across:

* Employees
* Events
* Locations

---

## Scope

Applies to:

* All organized play events
* All employees running or assisting with events
* All locations

---

## Relationship to Other Sections

* **Policies** define behavior and enforcement rules
* **Events** define how events are executed
* **Organized Play** defines standards that must be upheld during events

Employees must follow all three.

Do not override one section with another.

---

## Policy Enforcement During Events

All store policies apply at all times, including during events.

See:

* [Customer Conduct](../policies/customer_conduct.md)
* [Family-Friendly Environment](../policies/family_friendly.md)
* [Store Safety](../policies/store_safety.md)

### Requirements

* Policies must be enforced immediately during events
* Events do not change or relax store rules
* Employees may not ignore violations because an event is running

---

## Authority and Responsibility

* The assigned event lead or judge is responsible for running the event
* Employees running events are responsible for enforcement during the event
* Managers have final authority in all situations

Employees must escalate to a manager when required by policy.

---

## Consistency Across Locations

* Events must be run the same way at all locations
* This includes:

  * Enforcement
  * Player expectations
  * Application of rules

Employees may not create location-specific exceptions.

---

## Event Integrity

Employees must maintain control of the event at all times.

### Requirements

* Do not allow players to self-regulate disputes
* Do not allow inconsistent rulings
* Do not allow behavior that disrupts the event

All rulings must be made by the event lead or escalated appropriately.

---

## Event Timing

* Events must start at the scheduled time
* Do not delay events for late players

Late registration is allowed only if it does not disrupt the event.

* Players added late receive losses for missed rounds

---

## Player Removal

If a player is removed from an event:

* They are removed immediately
* They do not receive a refund
* Do not argue or negotiate

Follow policy procedures for removal.

---

## Minimum Event Requirements

* Some events require minimum player counts
* Requirements are determined per event

If minimum requirements are not met:

* Do not run the event if prohibited by the event rules

---

## System Compliance

Events must be run using the correct official system for each game.

Do not:

* Run events outside of required systems
* Skip required reporting or tracking

---

## Early Release Compliance

All early release rules must be followed.

* Do not sell products early unless explicitly allowed
* Do not violate publisher or distributor policies

Failure to comply may result in loss of privileges.

---

## Prize Support

All prize support must be tracked through the POS system.

* Process prize items as $0 transactions
* Do not distribute inventory without recording it

---

## Employee Conduct During Events

Employees must:

* Remain professional at all times
* Enforce rules consistently
* Avoid favoritism

Employees may not:

* Ignore violations
* Bend rules for specific players
* Create exceptions

---

## Summary

* Policies apply at all times
* Events do not override rules
* Employees must enforce standards immediately
* Events must be consistent across locations
* Systems and compliance are required

This document is enforceable. All employees must follow it.
