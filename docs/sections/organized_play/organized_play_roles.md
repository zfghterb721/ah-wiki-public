# Organized Play Roles

## Purpose

This document defines the roles and responsibilities of employees during organized play events.

Roles must be clearly understood and followed to ensure:
- Consistent event execution
- Clear authority
- Proper enforcement of rules and policies

---

## Scope

Applies to:
- All organized play events
- All employees involved in events
- All locations

---

## Role Overview

Organized play uses three roles:

- Judge / Event Lead
- Event Staff
- Manager

Each role has defined responsibilities. Do not assume responsibilities outside your role unless required.

---

## Judge / Event Lead

### Definition

The Judge/Event Lead is the employee assigned to run the event.

This role has primary control of the event.

---

### Responsibilities

- Run the event using the correct system
- Start and manage the event on time
- Make rulings and resolve disputes
- Enforce all store policies during the event
- Maintain control of the event environment
- Ensure fairness and consistency across all players
- Escalate issues when required

---

### Authority

- Has authority over all event-related decisions
- All disputes must go through the judge/event lead
- Players may not bypass this role

---

### Limitations

- Must escalate to a manager when required
- May not ignore policy violations
- May not create exceptions to rules

---

## Event Staff

### Definition

Event Staff are employees assisting with the event but not acting as the judge/event lead.

---

### Responsibilities

- Assist with player check-in and registration
- Help manage seating and space
- Support event flow (pairings, results, etc.)
- Monitor player behavior
- Report issues to the judge/event lead

---

### Authority

- May address minor issues immediately
- May enforce store policies as needed
- Must defer event rulings to the judge/event lead

---

### Limitations

- May not make final rulings on disputes
- May not override judge decisions
- Must escalate issues to the judge/event lead when necessary

---

## Manager

### Definition

The Manager oversees store operations and provides escalation support during events.

---

### Responsibilities

- Provide support when issues escalate beyond the judge/event lead
- Make final decisions when required
- Handle bans and serious disciplinary actions
- Ensure policies are enforced correctly
- Support staff in difficult or high-risk situations

---

### Authority

- Final authority in all situations
- May override decisions when necessary

---

### Involvement

Managers must be involved when:

- A player is removed from the event
- A ban is being considered
- A situation escalates beyond normal control
- Safety concerns are present
- Staff are uncertain how to proceed

---

## Role Interaction

### Judge → Manager

- Escalate when required
- Provide clear information about the situation
- Follow final decisions

---

### Staff → Judge

- Report issues immediately
- Do not attempt to resolve disputes independently
- Support event execution as directed

---

### Staff → Manager

- Escalate directly only when:
  - The judge/event lead is unavailable
  - The situation involves safety or immediate risk

---

## Player Interaction Rules

All roles must:

- Remain professional
- Communicate clearly and directly
- Avoid arguments or extended debate

Do not:

- Allow players to bypass authority structure
- Allow disputes to continue without resolution
- Show favoritism

---

## Enforcement Responsibility

All roles share responsibility for enforcement.

- Policies must be enforced at all times
- Issues must be addressed immediately
- Do not ignore violations

Failure to enforce policies is a violation.

---

## Playing in Events

- Employees may not play in events they are running unless explicitly approved

If approved:

- Responsibilities take priority over participation
- Event integrity must not be compromised

---

## Summary

- Judge/event lead runs the event and makes rulings
- Event staff support and escalate issues
- Manager provides final authority and escalation

All roles must:
- Enforce policies
- Maintain control
- Ensure consistency

This document is enforceable. All employees must follow it.
