# Attendance Policy

## Purpose

Consistent attendance is required for store operations. Every shift must be staffed and covered without disruption.

Failure to meet attendance expectations directly impacts coworkers, customers, and business operations.

---

## Attendance Expectations

- Employees must arrive **on time and ready to work** for all scheduled shifts
- Employees must remain **on-site for the full duration** of their shift unless approved by a manager
- Employees may not leave a shift early without **explicit manager approval**
- Employees may not leave one location to travel to another location without approval

---

## Time Tracking

- All time must be recorded using the **Workforce app**
- Employees must:
  - Clock in at the start of their shift
  - Clock out at the end of their shift
- Employees may not:
  - Clock in before arriving
  - Clock out after leaving
  - Record time for shifts not worked

Failure to accurately track time is a policy violation.

---

## Tardiness

- Employees are considered late if they are not clocked in **at their scheduled start time**
- Tardiness must be communicated **before the scheduled start time**

### Tardiness Thresholds

- 3 tardies within a 30-day period → **Written warning**
- 5 tardies within a 30-day period → **Final warning**
- Continued tardiness after final warning → **Termination**

---

## Call-Out Procedure

If an employee cannot work a scheduled shift:

- The employee must notify a manager **as soon as possible**
- Notification must be:
  - Direct (call, text, or Discord message to a manager)
  - Not sent through another employee
- Employees must provide:
  - The shift they are missing
  - The reason for absence

Failure to follow this process is treated as a **no-call/no-show**

---

## No-Call / No-Show

A no-call/no-show occurs when:

- An employee does not report to a scheduled shift
- AND does not notify a manager in advance

### No-Call / No-Show Enforcement

- First occurrence → **Final warning**
- Second occurrence → **Termination**

No-call/no-show is considered a severe violation.

---

## Shift Coverage

- Employees are responsible for their scheduled shifts
- Employees may request coverage, but:
  - A replacement must be approved by a manager
  - The shift remains the responsibility of the originally scheduled employee until approved
- Employees may not:
  - Trade shifts without approval
  - Assume coverage is accepted without confirmation

---

## Time-Off Requests

- Time-off requests must be submitted in advance
- Approval is not guaranteed
- Time off is not approved until confirmed by a manager

Employees may not:

- Assume time off is granted without approval
- Miss shifts due to unapproved time-off requests

---

## Leaving During a Shift

- Employees may not leave during a shift without manager approval
- This includes:
  - Personal errands
  - Traveling between store locations
  - Any “business-related” reason not explicitly approved

Unauthorized departure from a shift is a policy violation.

---

## Enforcement

Attendance violations include, but are not limited to:

- Tardiness
- Failure to follow call-out procedure
- No-call/no-show
- Leaving a shift without approval
- Failure to properly track time

### Escalation

Unless otherwise specified:

- First violation → **Verbal warning**
- Second violation → **Written warning**
- Third violation → **Final warning**
- Continued violations → **Termination**

Severity may accelerate escalation at management discretion.

---

## Summary

- Be on time
- Be present for your full shift
- Communicate early and directly
- Do not assume approval for anything

If there is any uncertainty, contact a manager before acting.
