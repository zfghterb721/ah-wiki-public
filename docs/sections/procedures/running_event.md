# Running an Event

Follow these steps to manage an event once it has started.

The goal is to keep the event organized, on time, and enjoyable for all participants.

---

## Start of Each Round

- ☐ Generate pairings  
- ☐ Announce pairings clearly  
- ☐ Start round timer  
- ☐ Ensure players move to correct tables  

---

## During the Round

- ☐ Monitor play area  
- ☐ Ensure players are following rules  
- ☐ Address questions and judge calls  
- ☐ Watch for slow play or delays  

Employees should remain visible and available throughout the event.

---

## Handling Questions and Rulings

- ☐ Listen to both players  
- ☐ Make a clear and fair ruling  
- ☐ Keep the event moving  

If unsure:
- Pause the match if needed  
- Ask another staff member or manager  

Do not guess on important rulings.

---

## Maintaining Order

- ☐ Enforce store policies during the event  
- ☐ Address profanity or inappropriate behavior  
- ☐ Ensure players are respectful  

Events must follow all store policies, including the family-friendly environment policy.

---

## Time Management

- ☐ Monitor remaining round time  
- ☐ Announce time warnings (e.g., 10 minutes remaining)  
- ☐ Call time when the round ends  

Ensure matches conclude in a timely manner.

---

## End of Round

- ☐ Collect match results  
- ☐ Verify results are entered correctly  
- ☐ Resolve any disputes before next round  

Do not begin the next round until results are confirmed.

---

## Between Rounds

- ☐ Generate next pairings  
- ☐ Announce pairings clearly  
- ☐ Keep event moving without unnecessary delays  

---

## Handling Issues

If problems arise:

- Stay calm and professional  
- Address the issue directly  
- Reinforce store policies  
- Involve a manager if necessary  

**Suggested response:**  
"I need to keep the event running smoothly. Let’s resolve this so we can continue."

---

## Player Behavior

- ☐ Address unsportsmanlike conduct immediately  
- ☐ Enforce family-friendly expectations  
- ☐ Intervene early before issues escalate  

Repeated or severe issues may result in removal from the event.

---

## Removal from Event

Employees may remove a participant for:

- Disruptive behavior  
- Failure to follow rules or instructions  
- Violating store policies  

If a player is removed:
- Inform them clearly  
- Document the situation  
- Notify a manager  

---

## Final Round

- ☐ Confirm final results are accurate  
- ☐ Announce final standings  

---

## Important Notes

- Do NOT allow the event to stall unnecessarily  
- Do NOT ignore behavior issues  
- Do NOT delay rulings—act promptly  

If unsure, ask a manager.

---

Running events effectively ensures a positive experience for players and reflects the professionalism of A&H Games.
