## Welcome to A&H Games!

### Mission Statement

Our mission at A&H Games is to provide North Louisiana's gaming community an opportunity to learn, play, and socialize within a fun, family-friendly environment. A&H Games takes pride in providing the best service and knowledge to various gaming and hobby enthusiasts.

# General Information

A&H Games is the operating name of Alvar and Hurrik's LLC. The use of the DBA name "A&H Games" is understood to refer to Alvar and Hurrik's LLC and any of its subsidiaries. Any unspecified use of the term "company" in this document is understood to refer to Alvar and Hurrik's LLC and its subsidiaries.

Each A&H Games employee is assigned to a specific A&H Games location to which their shifts take place. Hereinafter, "A&H Games location" is understood to refer to the A&H Games location to which the respective employees are assigned for their specific shifts.

The term "opening shift" is understood to refer to any shift which takes place at the posted opening time for the A&H Games location. The term "closing shift" is understood to refer to any shift which takes place at the posted closing time for the A&H Games location. The term "after hours" is understood to refer to the period of time between a closing shift at an A&H Games location and the next opening shift at that A&H Games location.

Each A&H Games location has a set of lights, hereinafter referred to as "security lights," which are left on at all hours for security purposes.

# Code of Conduct

Adherence to the Standard of Conduct as stated in this document is required for all A&H Games employees. Lack of familiarity with the Standard of Conduct will not excuse or protect employees from the penalties associated with violating the Standard of Conduct. An employee deemed to have violated any of the Standard of Conduct may undergo immediate disciplinary action which could result in termination of employment.

Each employee is required to conduct themselves in a manner that reflects the Mission Statement and ethics of A&H Games. Any employee who has been deemed to have engaged in one of the following activities or actions will be considered to be in violation of the Standard of Conduct.

-  Theft or inappropriate removal or possession of property;

-  Giving inappropriate discounts;

-  Falsification of timekeeping records;

-  Working under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs;

-  Possession, distribution, sale, transfer, or use of alcohol or illegal drugs in the workplace;

-  Fighting or threatening violence;

-  Harassment;

-  Boisterous or disruptive activity;

-  Negligence or improper conduct leading to property damage;

-  Insubordination or other disrespectful conduct;

-  Violation of safety or health rules;

-  Smoking or vaping inside the workplace;

-  Any absence or tardiness without notice;

-  Unauthorized use of company property;

-  Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information;

-  Violation of personnel policies; 

-  Unsatisfactory performance or conduct;

Determining whether or not an action falls within the scope of the prohibited actions listed above is the responsibility of A&H Games management.

# Policies and Procedures

## General

### Parking
Employees are permitted to park in the publicly accessible parking lot at each A&H Games location.

### Probationary Period for New Employees
A probation period for all employees will last up to 60 days from their respective dates of hire. During this time new employees have the opportunity to evaluate A&H Games as a place to work, and A&H Games management has the opportunity to ensure new employees are well-suited to satisfy business needs. A&H Games has the right to terminate employment without notice during this probationary period.

### Attendance
All employees are obligated to be present and prepared at the A&H Games location, behind the counter, at the exact commencement of their assigned shift. It is expected that employees will complete their entire shift for each designated workday.

Should an employee be unable to fulfill their scheduled shift due to unforeseen circumstances, they are required to personally inform their direct manager in a timely manner prior to the shift. Communication about absences should be made directly, with voicemail messages reserved only for extreme emergencies.

Tardiness or absence without prior approval may prompt immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

Employee work schedules can be accessed through Quickbooks Time at the following link: [https://tsheets.intuit.com/?realm_id=193514843494199&from=time_center](https://tsheets.intuit.com/?realm_id=193514843494199&from=time_center)

Adherence to these guidelines ensures the smooth operation of our store and is appreciated by the entire A&H Games team and community.

### Building Security
Any employee who is in possession of a company key (building, office, etc.) is responsible for its safekeeping. Upon receiving a key, each employee will sign a key disbursement form acknowledging their responsibilities as a keyholder. Security lights vary by location. It is the responsibility of each employee to confirm with a member of management which lights are designated as security lights for their respective A&H Games location.

All employees working during the closing shift are responsible for ensuring that all lights (except security lights) are turned off, thermostats are set to appropriate settings, and that all exterior doors are securely locked after the posted closing time for the A&H Games location. Employees or any other person(s) are not allowed on company premises after hours without prior authorization from a member of A&H Games management.

A&H Games assumes no risk or responsibility for any loss or damage to personal property.

### Smoking
Employees are not allowed to vape, or smoke, inside or in front of the A&H Games store. All vaping or smoking must take place in location specific designated smoking areas.

### Personnel Data Changes
It is the responsibility of each employee to promptly notify A&H of any of the following:
- Change of address
- Phone Number
- Individual to be contacted in the event of an emergency

### Inclement Weather/Emergency Closing
At times, emergencies such as severe weather, fires, or power failures can disrupt company operations. The decision to close the office will be made by the A&H leadership team and notified to you by your manager.

When the decision is made to close the store, employees will receive official notification through Signal.

Time off from scheduled work due to emergency closings will be unpaid unless otherwise specified.

### Corrective Action
A&H holds each of its employees to certain work rules and standards of conduct. When an employee deviates from these rules and standards, A&H expects the manager to take corrective action.

The usual sequence of corrective actions include an oral warning, a written warning, and finally termination of employment. Depending on the seriousness of the infractions, and the circumstances surrounding the matter, A&H has the right to terminate the employee without notice.

These include any of the infractions listed under the standards of conduct section above.

### Cell Phone and Internet Usage
A&H Games employees are allowed to use the internet and their own personal cell phones. Employees are not allowed on any inappropriate websites. Cell Phone usage shall not affect job duties, or interfere with customer interactions.


## Compensation and Benefits

### Payroll/Timekeeping
Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis Monday through Sunday. Paychecks are delivered via direct deposit into your bank account. Timekeeping is done using Quickbooks Time and is mandatory for all employees to track time.

### Time Off
To get time off you must inform the manager at least one week prior to taking time off and you must find some to cover the shift(s).

### Expense Reimbursement
Company expenses incurred by an employee must have prior approval by a manager. Reimbursements will be included in the employee's next regular paycheck or reimbursed via cash or check.

## On-the-Clock Gameplay
1.  **No Participation in Events or Casual Play on the Clock:** To ensure optimal store vigilance and service, employees are not permitted to participate in gaming events or engage in casual play while on duty.

2.  **Demo Games at the Counter Permitted:** Employees are encouraged to facilitate brief demonstration games at the counter for customers. This helps our customers understand gameplay mechanics and enhances the A&H Games shopping experience.

3.  **Facilitation of Tournaments Permitted:** While on the clock, employees are permitted and encouraged to facilitate and run tournaments. However, participation as a player in these tournaments is not allowed.

4.  **No "Clocking Out" Early to Play Games**\
    Communicate at least a day ahead of time with your manager if you are planning on participating in instore activities instead of working your scheduled shift. Showing up and not clocking in is the same as not showing up for shift even if you are in the same room.

This policy is designed to maintain a high level of service and attentiveness throughout all areas of the store, including the register area, retail area, play space, and with our customers. Always remember, every A&H Games employee has 'vigilance' and our battlefield is the store floor. We cannot afford to be 'tapped out' and leave our store defenseless while on duty. Balancing our passion for games with our professional responsibilities is key to delivering the best possible experience for our community.

## Store Area Access
At A&H Games, we have designated specific areas for employees and customers to ensure a smooth operation and safety for everyone:

1.  **Employee-Only Areas:** These include the area behind the counter and back storage rooms. To ensure the security and integrity of company property, customers are not permitted in these areas under any circumstances. Exceptions to this rule, if any, will be explicitly outlined in future policy updates.

2.  **Customer Areas:** These consist of the retail area, play space, and customer bathrooms. We encourage customers to freely explore these zones, where they can browse merchandise, participate in gameplay, and utilize restroom facilities.

By restricting customer access to certain areas, we protect both our staff and patrons from any suspicions or complications in case of an incident involving company property. This clear zoning of the store areas allows us to maintain a secure and pleasant gaming environment for everyone.

## Retail Purchasing
A&H Games takes pride in fostering a vibrant and dynamic gaming community. We offer the opportunity for customers to sell certain items to us, adhering to the following policies:

1.  **Eligible Items for Purchase:** A&H Games purchases Trading Card Game singles, video game consoles, accessories, games, and select tabletop miniatures from customers. These specific categories constitute the only items we are permitted to purchase. All other items not explicitly listed in our system are not eligible for purchase. Exceptions may be made solely with the explicit permission of store owners.

2.  **Cash and Store Credit:** Eligible items may be sold to us in exchange for cash or store credit. For cash transactions exceeding $50, a written check will be issued with the corresponding buylist or cart number noted.

3.  **Identification Requirement:** For all cash transactions, it is mandatory to record a valid driver's license number. This measure is in place in accordance with regulatory requirements and to ensure the safety of our transactions.

4.  **Employee Sales:** Should an employee wish to sell items to the store, the transaction must be processed by a different team member. This policy is to maintain transparency and avoid potential conflicts of interest, and there will be no exceptions.

Adherence to this policy is essential to maintain the integrity of our store's inventory and promotes fair and secure transactions. We appreciate your compliance.

## Soliciting and Advertising
At A&H Games, we aim to foster an inclusive gaming community. To maintain a conducive and focused environment, we have established the following policy regarding soliciting and advertising:

 - No advertising or solicitation of any kind is permitted within our store premises unless directly related to the gaming industry or community. Exceptions will only be granted by store ownership.

 - However, we acknowledge the value of community engagement and interaction. Therefore, customers wishing to personally invite others to join them for community events, religious gatherings, comic cons, events at other game stores, etc., are free to do so.

 - Customers may also post in the Discord about such invitations. We consider these posts as personal invitations from customers to other community members, and not a direct endorsement or sign-off by A&H Games on specific businesses, events, movements, etc.

Our goal is to create a diverse, neutral space for everyone to come together under the banner of gaming. We appreciate your understanding and adherence to this policy to ensure a comfortable and inclusive environment.
## Employee Inventory Use
At A&H Games, preserving the integrity of our inventory is of paramount importance for our operations and customer satisfaction. As such, the following guidelines have been established concerning the use of store inventory by employees:

1.  **No Personal Use of Inventory:** Employees are strictly prohibited from using store inventory for personal use without explicit permission from the owners. This includes the temporary borrowing of items such as singles or miniatures for a tournament or game. This policy ensures fairness to our customers and safeguards the consistency of our inventory.

2.  **Record Promotional Usage:** Employees are allowed to use store inventory for promotional activities such as prize support, product demonstrations, or good faith giveaways to customers. However, all items used for these purposes must be processed through the Point of Sale (POS) system, and the full value of the item recorded under the "Promotional" tender. This ensures we maintain accurate inventory and financial records. Note that no items used for these promotional activities should end up in the personal possession of an employee.

Failure to comply with this policy is regarded as a severe infraction. We trust all our team members to respect this policy for the efficient operation of our store and our commitment to fairness and customer satisfaction.

## Tips and Change Handling Policy

At A&H Games, we are committed to upholding the highest standards of integrity, transparency, and professionalism in every customer interaction. To reinforce this standard and maintain clarity in all financial transactions, the following policy regarding tips and change must be followed by all employees:

1. **Do Not Accept Tips or Pocket Change**  
   Employees are strictly prohibited from accepting cash, coins, or any form of gratuity from customers while on shift. Even if offered in good faith, accepting money from customers can be misinterpreted and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. This is a zero-tolerance policy.

2. **Customer Insists on Leaving Change**  
   If a customer says, “keep the change,” politely insist on returning it. Let them know that company policy does not allow employees to keep change. Always offer the exact amount of change due.

3. **Give-a-Penny / Take-a-Penny Jar**  
   If a customer refuses the coin change and leaves it anyway, place the change in the designated *Give-a-Penny / Take-a-Penny* jar near the register. This ensures that the funds are publicly accounted for and used as intended—to help other customers cover coin differences.

4. **Employee Use Prohibited**  
   Employees are not allowed to take coins from the *Give-a-Penny / Take-a-Penny* jar for any reason. It is for customer use only.

5. **Jar Amount Monitoring**  
   If the total amount of change in the jar ever exceeds **$10**, notify a manager. Management will determine the appropriate action to maintain accountability and prevent misuse.

6. **No Personal Retention of Money**  
   Under no circumstances should any employee place coins or bills into their pocket, bag, or personal space. Doing so—even if intended as temporary—may be interpreted as theft and used as grounds for immediate termination.

This policy is in place to protect both our employees and our company from any potential misunderstandings or allegations. It also helps preserve the trust and goodwill we’ve built with our community. Thank you for upholding this standard.

## Hygiene and Dress Code
At A&H Games, we prioritize providing a comfortable, safe, and pleasant atmosphere for our customers and staff. To uphold this standard, we have established the following guidelines regarding personal hygiene and dress code for all employees:

1.  **Personal Cleanliness:** Maintain personal cleanliness, including clean, well-groomed hair and fingernails.

2.  **Clothing:** Ensure that your clothes are clean, free of wrinkles, and freshly laundered. Never wear dirty or odorous clothing to work. Remember, you may be accustomed to the smell of your clothes, but others may not.

3.  **Dress Code:** The dress code is business VERY casual. Collared shirts, slacks, jeans, or shorts are preferred. T-shirts are acceptable, but avoid wearing athletic clothing or swimwear that could be perceived as unprofessional. You are encouraged to wear cool hats. Don't wear anything that has profanity or inappropriate writing/graphics. If you are wondering about it then don't wear it.

4.  **Smoke and Alcohol Odors:** Your personal appearance and scent should not reflect any use of cigarettes, alcohol, or marijuana. Remember, it is against company policy to work under the influence. Consider changing clothes before your shift or using odorless alternatives if smoking is part of your routine.

5.  **Deodorant:** Wear deodorant while on shift. Remember to reapply, particularly during hot weather or after physically intensive tasks. If necessary, consider bringing a change of clothes.

6.  **Fresh Breath:** Keep your breath fresh. If necessary, bring gum, mints, or mouthwash to work to maintain good oral hygiene throughout your shift.

7.  **Hand Hygiene:** Regular hand washing is mandatory, especially after using the restroom, eating, or touching your face.

8.  **Fragrances:** While wearing perfume or cologne is permitted, please ensure it's not overpowering. If the scent is strong enough to be disruptive or cause discomfort to others, we may need to address it.

9.  **Illness:** If you're exhibiting symptoms such as excessive sneezing, coughing, a runny nose, or other uncontrollable bodily functions, avoid coming to work. Not only do these symptoms create a less pleasant environment, but they can also signal illness, which should prevent you from coming to work altogether.

10. **Personal Items:** All personal items, including tattoos, piercings, or jewelry, are acceptable and should be maintained in a clean and neat manner.

Our goal is to create a clean, safe atmosphere that respects our customers' and colleagues' comfort. We trust each team member to embody this standard and set an overwhelmingly fresh example for everyone who visits A&H Games. Failure to adhere to this policy may result in disciplinary action.
