# Employment Expectations

This section defines how employees are expected to operate while working at A&H Games.

This is a customer-facing retail job. Employees are expected to prioritize the store, its customers, and its operations at all times while on shift.

Failure to meet these expectations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Serious violations may also fall under the [Code of Conduct](code_of_conduct.md).

---

## Core Responsibility

While on the clock, your responsibility is to:

- Serve customers  
- Maintain store operations  
- Protect inventory and company assets  

At no point should an employee behave as a customer while clocked in.

---

## Customers Come First

Customers are the top priority at all times.

Employees must:

- Acknowledge customers when they enter the store  
- Offer assistance when appropriate  
- Remain available and approachable  
- Stop non-essential tasks when a customer needs help  

Ignoring customers, delaying assistance, or prioritizing personal activity over customers is not acceptable.

---

## Work vs. Personal Activity

Employees are being paid to work—not to socialize, play games, or use the store for personal activity.

While on shift, employees may not:

- Participate in gameplay except as required for demonstrations or event operation  
- Engage in extended personal conversations that interfere with responsibilities  
- Use personal devices in a way that reduces awareness or availability  

Short, incidental interactions are acceptable. Anything that interferes with job performance is not.

---

## Store Awareness

Employees must maintain awareness of the entire store at all times.

This includes:

- Monitoring the front counter  
- Being aware of customers entering or leaving  
- Observing activity in the play space  
- Noticing potential issues (theft, disputes, safety concerns)  

Employees should not position themselves in ways that limit visibility or responsiveness.

---

## Ownership of Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for completing all assigned tasks and maintaining the store during their shift.

- Do not wait to be told to complete obvious tasks  
- Do not leave tasks incomplete for the next shift  
- Take initiative to maintain cleanliness, organization, and workflow  

If something needs to be done, do it.

---

## Professional Conduct on Shift

Employees must conduct themselves professionally at all times.

- Speak respectfully to customers and coworkers  
- Avoid inappropriate language or behavior  
- Remain calm and composed during stressful situations  

Employees represent A&H Games at all times while on shift.

---

## Use of Store Resources

Store resources exist for business use.

Employees may not use store property, inventory, or space for personal benefit unless explicitly permitted.

This includes:

- Inventory  
- Store space  
- Equipment  
- Time on the clock  

Unauthorized use may be treated as a policy violation and may fall under the [Code of Conduct](code_of_conduct.md).

---

## Following Policies and Procedures

Employees must follow all policies and procedures outlined in this handbook and related documentation.

- Policies define what is allowed  
- Procedures define how tasks are completed  

Failure to follow either is considered a violation of expectations and may escalate to a [Code of Conduct](code_of_conduct.md) violation depending on severity.

---

## Accountability

Employees are responsible for their actions and decisions while on shift.

- “I didn’t know” is not an acceptable excuse  
- When unsure, ask a manager before acting  
- Repeated mistakes or poor judgment may result in disciplinary action  

---

## Summary

- This is a job, not a hangout  
- Customers come first  
- Stay engaged and aware  
- Complete responsibilities without being told  
- Follow all policies and procedures  

These expectations apply at all times while on the clock.
