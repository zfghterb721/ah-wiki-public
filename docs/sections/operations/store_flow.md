# Store Flow

This document defines how employees operate during a shift.

It establishes:
- What employees must be doing at all times
- How attention is prioritized
- How to maintain control of the store without direction

This is not optional guidance. These expectations apply at all times.

---

## Core Priority Order

At all times, employees must prioritize in the following order:

1. Customers (engagement and assistance)
2. Transactions (active checkout)
3. Store Awareness (environment, behavior, safety)
4. Operational Tasks (cleaning, stocking, organizing)

Lower priorities must be immediately dropped when a higher priority requires attention.

---

## Constant Engagement Requirement

Employees must remain actively engaged at all times.

Employees may not:
- Stand idle
- Wait to be told what to do
- Remain focused on low-priority tasks when higher priorities exist

If no immediate task is obvious, employees must:
- Scan the store
- Identify issues
- Begin productive work immediately

There is always work to be done.

---

## Customer Control and Presence

Employees must maintain active control of the store environment.

This includes:
- Acknowledging every customer entering the store
- Maintaining visibility of both retail and play areas
- Positioning themselves where customers can easily approach

Employees may not:
- Become isolated behind the counter without awareness
- Ignore customers while performing tasks
- Allow customers to search for help

If a customer is in the store, employees must be aware of them.

---

## Register Priority

Transactions take immediate priority when a customer is ready to check out.

Employees must:
- Stop other tasks immediately
- Complete transactions accurately and efficiently
- Remain focused until the transaction is complete

Employees may not:
- Delay a customer at the register
- Continue unrelated tasks while a customer is waiting

After completing a transaction, employees must immediately return to active duties.

---

## Store Awareness

Employees must maintain awareness of the entire store at all times.

This includes:
- Retail floor condition
- Play space behavior and activity
- Cleanliness and organization
- Safety concerns

Employees must actively monitor, not passively notice.

Employees may not:
- Focus so narrowly on a task that awareness is lost
- Ignore developing issues in the store

---

## Task Execution

Tasks exist to support store function, not replace awareness.

Employees must:
- Perform tasks efficiently
- Pause tasks immediately when interrupted by higher priorities
- Resume tasks when available

Employees may not:
- Continue tasks while customers are waiting
- Treat tasks as fixed or uninterrupted work

---

## Slow Period Expectations

Slow periods are working periods.

Employees must:
- Clean all visible areas
- Organize shelves and displays
- Restock products
- Correct any disorder in the store

Employees may not:
- Use downtime as personal time
- Stand behind the counter without purpose
- Wait for instructions

If the store appears fully in order, employees must reassess and improve details.

---

## Busy Period Expectations

During high traffic:

Employees must:
- Prioritize speed and efficiency
- Keep lines moving
- Maintain control of customer flow
- Preserve basic store organization

Employees may delay:
- Deep cleaning
- Non-essential stocking
- Low-impact tasks

Accuracy is required at all times. Speed may not reduce transaction correctness.

---

## Events

During events, the store operates under elevated awareness requirements.

Employees must:
- Actively monitor the play area
- Enforce rules and policies immediately
- Keep rounds and activities moving
- Maintain control of player behavior and environment

Event management does not replace customer service.

Both must be maintained.

---

## Task Switching

Employees must switch tasks immediately when priorities change.

This includes:
- Stopping tasks without hesitation
- Assisting customers immediately
- Returning to previous tasks when available

Employees may not:
- Finish a task before helping a customer
- Ignore priority changes

---

## Prohibited Behavior

Employees may not:

- Ignore customers
- Remain idle
- Wait for direction when work is available
- Prioritize low-impact tasks over customer needs
- Lose awareness of the store environment

These behaviors are performance failures.

---

## Default Action Rule

If an employee does not know what to do, they must:

1. Identify the highest priority need in the store
2. Act on it immediately

If no need is obvious:
- Clean
- Organize
- Improve the store environment

Lack of direction is not an excuse for inactivity.

---

Consistent store flow ensures:
- Faster service
- Better customer experience
- Fewer operational issues
- Stronger store control

All employees are expected to maintain this standard at all times.
