# Daily Store Flow

This document outlines how the store operates throughout the day and how employees should prioritize their time and attention.

The goal is to ensure consistent service, a clean environment, and smooth operations at all times.

---

## Core Priority

At all times, employee priority should be:

1. Customers  
2. Transactions  
3. Store Environment  
4. Secondary Tasks  

If multiple things are happening, focus on the highest priority first.

---

## Customer Priority

Customers always come first.

- Greet customers as they enter  
- Be available for questions and assistance  
- Stay aware of customers in the store  

Do not become so focused on tasks that customers are ignored.

---

## Register Priority

The register takes priority when a customer is ready to check out.

- Stop other tasks when needed  
- Complete transactions accurately  
- Return to other duties after the transaction  

---

## Store Awareness

Employees should maintain awareness of the entire store at all times.

- Monitor the retail area  
- Monitor the play space  
- Watch for issues (mess, behavior, safety)  

Avoid becoming isolated or distracted.

---

## During Slow Periods

When the store is not busy:

- Clean and organize the store  
- Restock inventory  
- Straighten shelves and displays  
- Review procedures or tasks  

There is always something productive to do.

---

## During Busy Periods

When the store is busy:

- Focus on customers and transactions  
- Keep lines moving  
- Maintain basic organization  
- Delay non-essential tasks  

Speed and awareness are important, but accuracy still matters.

---

## Events

During events:

- Prioritize event management  
- Monitor players and play area  
- Keep rounds moving  
- Enforce store policies  

Balance event needs with general customer service.

---

## Task Switching

Employees should be comfortable switching between tasks.

- Pause tasks when customers need help  
- Resume tasks when available  
- Keep track of what was in progress  

Do not become stuck on a single task.

---

## Important Notes

- Do NOT ignore customers while completing tasks  
- Do NOT remain idle when work is available  
- Do NOT prioritize low-impact tasks over customer needs  

If unsure what to do, ask a manager or refer to this priority system.

---

Understanding store flow ensures that employees focus on what matters most and helps create a smooth, consistent experience for customers.
