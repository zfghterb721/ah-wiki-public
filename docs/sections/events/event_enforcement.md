# Event Enforcement

Event enforcement must be immediate, consistent, and unbiased. Employees are responsible for maintaining order, fairness, and integrity at all times.

Failure to enforce rules is a failure of job performance.

Employees are expected to act decisively. Avoiding enforcement due to discomfort is not acceptable.

---

## Core Rules

- All rules must be **enforced consistently**
- Enforcement must be **immediate**
- All players must be treated **equally**
- Employees must remain **calm and direct**
- Managers make **final decisions on escalations**

Employees may not:

- Ignore violations
- Delay enforcement
- Make exceptions for specific customers
- Allow players to self-enforce rules

---

## Enforcement Standards

Employees must:

- Address issues as soon as they occur
- Speak clearly and directly
- State the issue and required correction
- Apply consequences consistently

Employees may not:

- Argue with players
- Apologize for enforcing rules
- Soften or weaken enforcement language

---

## Types of Violations

### Minor Violations

Examples include:

- Slow play
- Failure to follow event procedures
- Minor disruptions
- Incomplete or incorrect result reporting

Response:

- Issue a clear verbal warning
- Explain the correct behavior
- Monitor for repeat behavior

---

### Major Violations

Examples include:

- Unsportsmanlike conduct
- Repeated minor violations
- Arguing with staff
- Disruptive behavior affecting others

Response:

- Issue a firm warning or penalty
- Clearly state consequences of further issues
- Document the incident if necessary

---

### Severe Violations

Examples include:

- Cheating or suspected cheating
- Harassment or abusive behavior
- Threats or intimidation
- Refusal to follow staff instructions

Response:

- Stop the match or remove the player from the event
- Escalate immediately to a manager
- Document the incident

---

## Warning Structure

Employees must use a consistent escalation structure:

1. Verbal warning
2. Final warning (if behavior continues)
3. Removal from event

Employees may skip steps for major or severe violations.

Warnings must be:

- Clear
- Direct
- Documented when appropriate

---

## Player Removal

A player may be removed if they:

- Continue behavior after warnings
- Commit a major or severe violation
- Refuse to follow instructions

### Removal Procedure

- Inform the player they are removed from the event
- Do not debate the decision
- Direct the player to leave the event area

If the player refuses:

- Escalate to a manager immediately

Employees may not:

- Allow removed players to continue participating
- Reverse removal decisions without manager approval

---

## Handling Disputes

Employees must:

- Take control of the situation immediately
- Separate players if necessary
- Gather facts from both sides
- Make a ruling based on rules and policy

Employees may not:

- Allow arguments to continue unchecked
- Delay decisions unnecessarily

If unsure:

- Pause the match
- Escalate to a manager

---

## Cheating and Suspicion

If cheating is suspected:

- Pause the match immediately
- Do not accuse without basis
- Gather information
- Escalate to a manager

If cheating is confirmed:

- Remove the player from the event
- Follow management direction for further action

---

## Maintaining Authority

Employees must:

- Speak with confidence
- Maintain control of tone and environment
- Avoid emotional responses

Employees may not:

- Be intimidated by players
- Allow players to challenge authority without response

---

## Escalation to Management

Employees must escalate when:

- A player refuses to comply
- A situation becomes heated or disruptive
- A ruling may significantly impact fairness
- Cheating is suspected or confirmed
- Removal from the store may be required

---

## Documentation

Employees must document incidents when:

- A player is removed
- A severe violation occurs
- A situation may require follow-up

Documentation must include:

- Date and time
- Players involved
- Description of incident
- Actions taken

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- No ignoring violations
- No inconsistent enforcement
- No exceptions for familiarity or status
- No allowing arguments to escalate
- No failure to escalate serious issues

Failure to enforce rules is a performance issue.
