# Event Philosophy

Events at A&H Games are structured, controlled experiences. They are not casual gatherings.

Employees are responsible for maintaining order, enforcing rules, and ensuring every event runs consistently from start to finish.

The goal is not just to run events — it is to run them **correctly, predictably, and professionally**.

---

## Core Principles

All events must follow these principles:

- **Fairness** — All players are treated equally
- **Consistency** — Rules are applied the same way every time
- **Control** — Employees actively manage the event environment
- **Clarity** — Expectations are communicated clearly
- **Accountability** — Employees enforce rules without hesitation

These principles are non-negotiable.

---

## Events Are Controlled Environments

Events are not self-managed.

Employees must:

- Direct the flow of the event
- Maintain awareness of the room at all times
- Intervene immediately when issues arise

Employees may not:

- Allow players to manage their own disputes
- Allow the event to become disorganized
- Ignore problems as they develop

---

## Consistency Over Preference

- Rules must be applied the same way to every player
- Personal relationships must not affect decisions
- Familiar customers do not receive special treatment

Employees may not:

- Make exceptions for regulars or friends
- Adjust rulings based on player attitude
- Apply rules inconsistently

---

## Enforcement Is Required

Employees are expected to enforce rules even when uncomfortable.

Employees must:

- Address issues immediately
- Deliver rulings clearly and confidently
- Follow enforcement procedures without hesitation

Employees may not:

- Avoid enforcement to prevent conflict
- Delay action in difficult situations
- Allow behavior to continue unchecked

---

## No Improvisation

Event structure must be followed exactly as defined.

Employees may not:

- Modify event rules mid-event
- Change prize structures
- Adjust entry requirements
- Create “on-the-fly” solutions

If a situation is unclear:

- Pause the situation if necessary
- Escalate to a manager

---

## Player Experience

A good event experience is:

- Organized
- Predictable
- Fair
- Efficient

Employees are responsible for ensuring:

- Events start on time
- Rounds progress without unnecessary delay
- Players understand expectations

Employees may not:

- Allow confusion about rules or structure
- Let events fall behind schedule unnecessarily

---

## Behavior Standards

Players are expected to:

- Follow event rules
- Respect staff decisions
- Maintain appropriate conduct

Employees must:

- Address violations immediately
- Maintain a respectful but firm tone

Bad behavior must not be tolerated.

---

## Authority and Escalation

Employees are the authority during events.

Employees must:

- Make rulings when required
- Maintain control of situations
- Escalate to management when necessary

Managers have final authority on:

- Disputes
- Player removal
- Rule interpretation
- Exceptions

---

## Non-Negotiable Expectations

- Events must be controlled at all times
- Rules must be enforced consistently
- No exceptions based on familiarity
- No improvisation of rules or structure
- No ignoring problems

Failure to follow event philosophy is a performance issue.
