# Event Reporting

Event reporting is required for all events. Employees must document results, issues, and key details to ensure accountability, consistency, and cross-location visibility.

If it is not recorded, it did not happen.

---

## Core Rules

- Every event must be **recorded and finalized in the system**
- All issues must be **documented accurately**
- Reporting must be completed **immediately after teardown**
- Employees may not **skip or delay reporting**

---

## Required Reporting

After each event, employees must record:

- Event type
- Date and time
- Number of participants
- Entry fee
- Prize structure used
- Final standings (top placements at minimum)

This information must be:

- Entered into the event system (if applicable)
- Recorded in any required tracking logs

---

## Incident Reporting

Employees must document any notable issues, including:

- Player disputes
- Rule enforcement actions
- Warnings issued
- Player removals
- Suspected or confirmed cheating
- Disruptive or inappropriate behavior

### Required Details

Each incident report must include:

- Date and time
- Players involved
- Description of the issue
- Actions taken
- Whether management was involved

Employees may not:

- Omit details to simplify reporting
- Leave incidents undocumented

---

## Operational Issues

Employees must report operational problems, including:

- Delayed start times
- Space constraints or overcrowding
- Product shortages
- System or software issues
- Staffing issues

These reports are used to improve future events and must be accurate.

---

## Cross-Location Communication

Because events occur at multiple locations:

- Significant issues must be **communicated across locations**
- Patterns of behavior (problem players, repeated issues) must be **shared with management**

Employees must:

- Escalate recurring problems
- Ensure other staff are aware when necessary

---

## Data Accuracy

Employees must ensure all reported data is:

- Accurate
- Complete
- Consistent with actual event outcomes

Employees may not:

- Guess or estimate critical information
- Leave required fields incomplete

---

## Escalation Requirements

Employees must escalate to management when:

- A player is removed
- Cheating is suspected or confirmed
- A dispute could impact fairness or reputation
- An issue may require follow-up action

---

## Timing Requirements

- Reporting must be completed **immediately after teardown**
- Incidents must be documented **as soon as possible**

Employees may not:

- Delay reporting until a later shift
- Rely on memory for later documentation

---

## Storage and Access

All reports must be:

- Stored in designated systems or logs
- Accessible to management

Employees may not:

- Store reports privately
- Use informal or untracked notes as official records

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- No skipped reports
- No undocumented incidents
- No inaccurate reporting
- No delayed reporting

Failure to complete event reporting is a performance issue.
