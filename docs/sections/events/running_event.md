# Running an Event

Employees must actively manage events from start to finish. Events do not run themselves.

Employees are responsible for maintaining control of the room, enforcing rules, and ensuring the event progresses on schedule.

Failure to control an event results in poor customer experience and operational issues.

---

## Core Rules

- Events must remain **on schedule**
- Employees must maintain **visible control of the room**
- All players must be treated **consistently**
- Rules must be **enforced immediately**
- Employees may not allow players to **self-govern disputes**
- Employees may not **improvise rulings or procedures**

---

## Player Check-In

Before the event begins:

- All players must:
  - Be registered
  - Have paid entry fees
  - Be present

Employees must:

- Confirm all players are accounted for
- Remove no-shows before pairings are generated

Employees may not:

- Add players after the event has started
- Hold spots for late arrivals

---

## Event Start

- Announce clearly:
  - Event start
  - Format
  - Number of rounds (if known)
  - Time per round
  - Any critical rules

- Start the first round immediately after announcement

Employees must not delay the event for late players.

---

## Round Management

### Starting a Round

- Announce pairings clearly
- Ensure players can find their seats/opponents quickly
- Start the round timer immediately

### During the Round

Employees must:

- Remain present and attentive
- Monitor matches for:
  - Slow play
  - Disputes
  - Unsportsmanlike conduct
- Answer judge calls promptly

Employees may not:

- Leave the event unattended
- Ignore raised hands or disputes

---

## Time Enforcement

- Round time must be strictly enforced

### End of Round

- Announce time clearly
- Instruct players on end-of-round procedures (if applicable)

Employees must:

- Ensure matches conclude properly
- Prevent excessive overtime

Employees may not:

- Extend round time without manager approval
- Allow matches to continue indefinitely

---

## Late Players

Late players are not permitted once the event has started.

If a player arrives after start:

- They may not be added to the event
- Direct them to the next available event

No exceptions without manager approval.

---

## Match Results

- Players are responsible for reporting results
- Employees must ensure results are entered correctly

Employees must:

- Verify unclear or suspicious results
- Resolve discrepancies immediately

Employees may not:

- Guess or assume results
- Leave matches unreported

---

## Between Rounds

Employees must:

- Start the next round promptly after all results are entered
- Minimize downtime between rounds
- Keep players informed of timing

Employees may not:

- Allow unnecessary delays
- Wait for players who are not present

---

## Dispute Handling

- All disputes must be handled by staff

Employees must:

- Listen to both players
- Make a ruling based on event rules and game rules
- Deliver rulings clearly and confidently

Employees may not:

- Allow players to resolve disputes themselves
- Avoid making a ruling

If unsure:

- Pause the match
- Escalate to a manager immediately

---

## Player Conduct

Employees must actively monitor behavior.

Address immediately:

- Unsportsmanlike conduct
- Disruptive behavior
- Harassment or arguments
- Cheating or suspected cheating

Employees must:

- Intervene immediately
- Issue warnings or escalate as needed

Employees may not:

- Ignore behavior issues
- Delay enforcement

---

## Maintaining Control

Employees must:

- Stay visible and accessible
- Keep noise and disruption under control
- Ensure players remain focused on matches

If the environment becomes disorderly:

- Reassert control immediately
- Address individuals causing issues

---

## Drops and No-Shows

- Players who leave must be dropped from the event

Employees must:

- Confirm drops before removing players
- Remove inactive players before the next round

Employees may not:

- Leave inactive players in the system
- Pair players who are not present

---

## End of Event

- Confirm all final results are entered
- Verify standings before announcing winners

Employees must:

- Ensure accuracy before prize distribution
- Resolve any disputes before finalizing

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- No adding players after event start
- No delaying rounds unnecessarily
- No ignoring disputes or behavior issues
- No leaving events unattended
- No altering results without cause

Failure to properly run an event is a performance issue.
