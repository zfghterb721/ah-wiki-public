# Manager Accountability

## Purpose

Managers are responsible for enforcing all standards, policies, and procedures at all times.

Managers are held to a higher standard than employees.

Failure to enforce standards is a management failure.

---

## Core Responsibility

Managers are accountable for:

- Employee performance during their shift
- Enforcement of all policies and procedures
- Store operations and conditions
- Customer experience
- Incident handling and resolution
- Consistency across all decisions

Managers are responsible for outcomes, not intent.

---

## Ownership of the Shift

The manager on duty is fully responsible for the store.

This includes:

- All employee actions
- All customer interactions
- All operational conditions
- All incidents that occur

Managers may not:

- Shift blame to employees
- Claim lack of awareness as justification
- Ignore issues that occur under their supervision

If it happens during the shift, the manager is responsible.

---

## Enforcement Responsibility

Managers must:

- Enforce all policies and procedures consistently
- Correct violations immediately
- Escalate issues when required
- Prevent repeat behavior

Managers may not:

- Allow violations to go unaddressed
- Delay enforcement
- Choose when to enforce rules
- Ignore issues due to discomfort or inconvenience

Inconsistent enforcement is a management failure.

---

## Documentation Responsibility

Managers must:

- Document all corrections, incidents, and disciplinary actions
- Complete documentation the same day
- Ensure documentation is accurate and complete

Managers may not:

- Skip documentation
- Delay documentation
- Provide vague or incomplete records

If it is not documented, it did not happen.

---

## Consistency Between Managers

All managers must operate under the same standards.

Managers must:

- Align with existing policies and decisions
- Communicate with other managers when issues arise
- Ensure consistency across both locations

Managers may not:

- Create conflicting standards
- Make decisions that contradict policy
- Allow location-based differences in enforcement

Differences in enforcement must be corrected immediately.

---

## Handling Disagreements Between Managers

If managers disagree:

- The issue must be addressed directly between managers
- Resolution must occur quickly
- A consistent standard must be established

Managers may not:

- Present conflicting decisions to employees or customers
- Undermine another manager in front of others
- Allow disagreements to create inconsistency

Unified standards are required.

---

## Failure to Enforce

Failure to enforce includes:

- Ignoring policy violations
- Allowing repeated issues without escalation
- Failing to correct employee behavior
- Allowing operational standards to decline
- Failing to take control of incidents

Any of the above constitutes a management failure.

---

## Visibility and Engagement

Managers must:

- Remain active on the floor
- Maintain awareness of all store activity
- Intervene when necessary

Managers may not:

- Remain disengaged
- Stay isolated from operations
- Rely on employees to manage the store

Lack of presence is a failure of oversight.

---

## Escalation to Ownership

Ownership must be informed when:

- Serious incidents occur
- Employees require termination consideration
- Patterns of issues persist
- Manager-level concerns arise

Managers must communicate clearly and promptly.

---

## Performance Expectations

Managers are expected to:

- Maintain consistent standards at all times
- Make clear and decisive decisions
- Support and enforce employee accountability
- Prevent problems before they escalate

Managers must not operate reactively.

---

## Corrective Action for Managers

If a manager fails to meet expectations:

- The issue must be addressed directly
- The failure must be documented
- Expectations must be clearly restated

Repeated failures may result in:

- Loss of responsibilities
- Further disciplinary action
- Removal from management role

---

## Prohibited Practices

Managers may not:

- Ignore problems
- Delay action
- Enforce rules inconsistently
- Avoid difficult situations
- Undermine store standards
- Allow personal preference to influence decisions

---

## Summary

Managers are responsible for:

- Enforcing all standards without exception
- Maintaining full control of store operations
- Ensuring consistency across all employees and locations
- Documenting all relevant actions
- Taking responsibility for all outcomes

If standards are not upheld, management failed to perform their role.
